Hủ Tiếu

Fact: This Southern Vietnamese noodle dish is the cousin of pho, and it takes on a variety of different styles including Hu Tieu Nam Vang (Phnom Penh style) and Hu Tieu My Tho (after the city located in the Mekong Delta region of southern Vietnam) (Loving Pho). It can also be eaten wet or dry (as seen here).

Pro tip: Add boiled bean sprouts and tép mỡ (fried pork fat) for added flavor.
