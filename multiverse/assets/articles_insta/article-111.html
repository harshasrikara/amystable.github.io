Every year, from April to June, typhoons hit Hong Kong, and Causeway Bay was the largest typhoon shelter that protected merchants and fishermen from the storms. Where people gather, you get culinary innovations, and typhoon-style cooking was born. This style consists of covering proteins in breadcrumbs and frying the food.

Pro tip: Besides the breadcrumbs, add charred garlic and onions over a bed of crab fried rice to eat with your crab.
