Agedashi Tofu 揚げ出し豆腐

Fact: Agedashi tofu, meaning “lightly deep-fried tofu”. The cubed tofu achieves its crunchy-chewy skin from cornstarch or potato starch and is served in a tentsuyu, a broth combining dashi, mirin, and Japanese soy sauce. The dish is finally garnished with shredded seaweed and negi, green onions.

Pro tip: It’s difficult for the tofu to fully absorb the flavors of the tensuyu through the skin, so break the skin a bit at the top and scoop some of the tentsuyu into the tofu. With the deep fried skin holding the broth and tofu together, you have a little water bomb of flavors.
