Mì Quảng or Quảng-style Noodles

Fact: Another popular noodle dish from central Vietnam, Mì Quảng originated from the Quảng Nam Province. It consists of one or more proteins of shrimp, pork, beef, and fish. The noodles get their distinctive yellow color from turmeric which is added, and the soup derives it’s aroma from a variety of herb such as coriander, garlic, cilantro, and shallots.

Pro tip: Eat it with grilled rice crackers, bánh tráng nướng
