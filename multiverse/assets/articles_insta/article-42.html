Bánh Bột Chiên (Vietnamese Pan-Fried Rice Flour Cakes)

Fact: This is one of Vietnam’s iconic street foods, often sold in little food carts along the streets. In southern Vietnam, it’s a popular after-school snack for students. This dish consists of fried flour cakes held together with fried eggs and topped with vegetables and dried shrimps.

Pro tip: This dish is served with a tangy soy sauce that holds true to the sweet, sour, and savory flavor profiles common in Vietnamese cuisine.
